Part 1: Read the text – "Sarah's Busy Weekend"

Read the text about Sarah. The target grammar is in bold.

 

It’s Friday afternoon, and Sarah is looking at her calendar. She has a very busy weekend planned!

On Saturday morning, she isn't working. She's free! At 10 a.m., she is meeting her best friend, Tom, for coffee. They go to the same cafe every week. In the afternoon, she is playing tennis with her sister. They have a court booked for 3 p.m.

 

Saturday evening is special. She is going to the cinema with her boyfriend, Mark. They want to see a new comedy film.

 

Sunday is also full. In the morning, she is visiting her grandmother. She doesn't see her very often. For lunch, she is having pizza with her brother. Then, on Sunday evening, she is studying because she has an exam on Monday morning. She isn't going out on Sunday night.

 

Later, her friend texts her: "Are you doing anything on Sunday afternoon?" Sarah replies: "Yes, I am. I'm having lunch with my brother. What time are you meeting Lisa?"

 

It's a busy weekend, but Sarah is excited!

 

Part 2: Understanding the meaning

A. Answer the questions about the sentences from the text.

 

1. Sentence: "On Saturday morning, she is meeting her best friend, Tom, for coffee at 10 a.m."

●      Is she having coffee with Tom right now? (Yes / No)

●      Is this a plan for her future (Saturday morning)? (Yes / No)

●      Is she 100% sure about this plan? (Yes / No)

●      Does she have Tom's agreement to meet? (Yes / No)

 

2.  Sentence: "She isn't going out on Sunday night."

●      Is she staying at home on Sunday night? (Yes / No)

●      Is this a plan for her future? (Yes / No)

 

3.  Sentence (from the text message): "What time are you meeting Lisa?"

●      Does this question ask about the past, present, or future? (Past / Present / Future)

●      Does the person want to know about a fixed arrangement? (Yes / No)

Part 4: Complete the Rules

Fill in the gaps to complete the grammar rules.

 

1. We use the present continuous to talk about ______________ (things that are fixed/arranged) in the ______________ (near future).

 

2. Positive form (Example: She is meeting Tom.):

    Subject + _______________(to be) + main verb with _______________ (-ing)

 

3. Negative form (Example: She isn't working.):

    Subject + to be + _______________(not) + main verb with _______________(-ing)

 

4. Yes/No Question (Example: Are you doing anything?):

     _______________(To be) + subject + main verb with _______________(-ing)?

 

5. Wh- Question (Example: What time are you meeting Lisa?):

    Question word + _______________(to be) + subject + main verb with _______________(-ing)?

  

Part 5: Practice (Concept Checking Questions)

Choose the best answer (A, B, or C) to show you understand the meaning.*

 

1. "I am visiting my grandmother on Sunday."

This means:

    a) I am at my grandmother's house now.

    b) I have a plan to go to my grandmother's house on Sunday. ✅

    c) I want to visit my grandmother someday, but I'm not sure when.

 

2. "We are playing football at 3 pm."

This means:

    a) The game is happening right now.

    b) We play football every day at 3 pm.

    c) We have a fixed plan to play football later at 3 pm. ✅

 

3. "He isn't coming to the party."

This means:

    a) He is not at the party right now.

    b) He doesn't like parties.

    c) His plan is to stay home; he will not be at the party. ✅

 

4. "Are you studying tonight?"

This asks about:

    a) Your habits.

    b) Your future plans. ✅

    c) What you are doing at this exact moment.

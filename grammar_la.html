TARGET LANGUAGE

What is the language point?

3rd Conditional

 

Example(s):

If Peter had completed the course, he would have got a promotion.

Meaning analysis

The Third Conditional is used to describe hypothetical past situations that didn’t happen. Both the condition and the result are impossible to change because they are in the past.

Elicitation: meaning

What context will you use to elicit the language from Ss? Be specific and include the questions you are going to use for elicitation.

 

Context:

I would tell students a story about Peter, who had the opportunity to complete a professional course, but he didn’t finish it. Later, the company offered a promotion, but only to employees who had completed the course. Peter did not get the promotion. Pinpoint the fact that this happened in the past and cannot be changed. Then, Peter’s colleagues discussed the situation in terms of opposite results for Peter.

Elicitation question:

 

How could Peter’s colleagues describe this past situation, imagining a different result for Peter?

Concept checking

Concept Checking Questions or other techniques to be used to check Ss’ understanding

 

Checking past time

Is the situation with Peter happening now? (No)

Did Peter finish the professional course? (No)

Did the company give promotions? (Yes)

Did Peter receive the promotion? (No)

 

Checking impossibility

Can Peter get that same promotion now? (No)

 

Checking imaginary result

Are his colleagues talking about the real result or a different imagined result? (Imagined result)

In their discussion, does Peter get the promotion? (Yes)

Did that imagined result really happen or can happen? (No)

 

Form

Form Analysis (think also about changes that take place in question forms, negatives and short answers)

 

If Peter had completed the course, he would have got a promotion.

If + Subject + had + V3, + Subject + would have (would’ve) + V3

Or

Peter would have got a promotion if he had completed the course.

Subject + would have (would’ve) + V3 + if + subject + had + V3

 

If Peter had not completed the course, he would not have got a promotion.

If + Subject + had not (hadn’t) + V3, + Subject + would not have (wouldn’t have) + V3

Or

Peter would not have got a promotion if he had not completed the course.

Subject + would not have (wouldn’t have) + V3 + if + subject + had not (hadn’t) + V3

 

Would Peter have got a promotion if he had completed the course?

Would + Subject + have + V3 + if + Subject + had + V3?

Or

If Peter had completed the course, would he have got a promotion?

If + Subject + had + V3, would + Subject + have + V3?

 

Written Record

The target language will be highlighted in the reading and listening text before the grammar focus stage.

On the board I will write the model sentence and underline key elements:

had completed + would have got

I will colour-code:

If + past perfect → would have + V3

Students will copy the structure and example sentence into their notebooks.

ANTICIPATED

PROBLEMS & SOLUTIONS:

 

MEANING

Problem 1: Ss may confuse the Third Conditional with the Second Conditional and say If Peter completed the course, he would have got a promotion.

Solution 1:
Draw a timeline clearly showing both actions in the past.
Ask CCQs focusing on time reference (Is this about the past or present?).
Contrast with a Second Conditional example and highlight the tense difference.

 

Problem 2: Ss may think Peter still has the opportunity to complete the course.

Solution 2:

Make it clear in the context that the opportunity is finished.
Ask CCQs: Can Peter complete the course now and change the result? (No)
Reinforce the idea of regret or missed opportunity.

ANTICIPATED

PROBLEMS & SOLUTIONS:

 

FORM

Problem 1: Ss may replace structure elements, e.g. “If Peter would have completed the course…”

Solution 1:
Board the incorrect example and cross out “would” in the if-clause.
Elicit that “would” is only used in the main clause.
Highlight: If + had + V3.

 

Problem 2: Ss may forget “have” after “would” (he would got a promotion).

Solution 2:
Underline the full chunk: would have V3.
Drill the chunk as one unit.
Correct mistakes during controlled practice.

ANTICIPATED

PROBLEMS & SOLUTIONS:

 

PHONOLOGY

Problem 1: Ss may pronounce “would have” with strong forms and unnatural rhythm.

Solution 1:
Model and drill the sentence.
Board the phonemic script.
Drill “would have got” as a chunk.

 

Problem 2: Ss may struggle with the long sentence when drilling.

Solution 2:
Use backchaining:

a promotion → got a promotion → have got a promotion → would have got a promotion → he would have got a promotion.

Drill chorally and individually, focusing on rhythm and sentence stress.

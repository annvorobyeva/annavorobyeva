housework

/ˈhaʊsˌwɜːk/

 

noun (uncountable)

 

Stress on HOUSEwork

 

Work that needs to be done regularly in the home, such as cleaning, cooking, washing clothes.

Context: I show a picture of a family at home: the mother is washing dishes, the father is vacuuming, the child is making the bed.

 

Eliciting questions:

• What is the mother doing? (washing dishes)

• What is the father doing? (cleaning / vacuuming)

• Do we do these things at home or at work? (at home)

• Is there one word for cleaning, cooking and washing at home? (housework)

CCQs:

1. Do we do housework at home? (Yes)
This checks understanding of place.

2. Is it something you do for fun, like playing video games? (No)
This checks that housework is not an activity for pleasure.

3. Is homework housework? (No)
This checks distinction between housework and homework.

4. Do we get paid for housework? (No)
This checks that housework is unpaid.

5. Is housework one job or many small jobs? (Many small jobs)
This checks that housework includes different activities.

6. Is cooking housework? (Yes)
This checks understanding of included activities.

7. Do these actions help us keep the home clean?
(Yes)
This checks the purpose of housework.

8. Is it done regularly?
(Yes)
This checks frequency and routine nature.

 

Meaning: Students may confuse housework with homework.

Solution: Compare both words on the board and ask CCQs.

 

Form: Students may try to say a housework.

Solution: Clarify that housework is uncountable and give correct examples (a lot of housework).

 

Pronunciation: Students may stress the second syllable (houseWORK).

Solution: Mark stress clearly, model and drill chorally and individually, use finger highlighting.

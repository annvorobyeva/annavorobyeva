Our lesson, dedicated to the Walt Disney Company, is held to achieve some essential goals, which we, as the teachers, hope to reach. We can split the objectives into three groups:

Main aim:

Introduce students the history of Disney company, its eras of animated films, take a closer look at Disney legendary characters.

We aim to enhance students' advanced English communication skills through engaging discussions and collaborative activities centered on the history, evolution, and cultural impact of Disney.

Subsidiary aim

  1. Vocabulary expansion

We aim to expand students' vocabulary related to cinematography, animation, film history, cultural studies, and to practice applying this vocabulary in contextually relevant scenarios.

  1. Critical skills’ development

We aim to develop students' ability to critically analyze Disney's films and characters from various perspectives and to formulate well-supported arguments.

  1. Collaborative communication and teamwork

We aim to improve students' ability to work effectively in groups, share ideas, negotiate viewpoints, and present their findings in a clear and coherent manner.

  1. Historical understanding

We aim to enhance students' understanding of the historical context surrounding the creation and evolution of Disney.

  1. Creative expression

 We aim to encourage students to express their own creativity and imagination through work inspired by Disney world.

Teacher’s aim

  1. To facilitate a dynamic and engaging learning environment that fosters active participation and critical thinking.
  2. To provide students with meaningful and relevant opportunities to practice their English skills in a context that is both enjoyable and at times challenging.
  3. To assess students' understanding of the topic and their progress in developing their English skills.
  4. To create a positive and collaborative learning atmosphere where students feel comfortable taking risks and expressing their ideas.
  5. To make the lesson interesting to students and help them to discover something new about Disney.

Lesson plan:

  1. Activity #1: Questions on the slide (3-5 minutes)
  • What associations do you have when you mention "Disney"?
  • Which Disney cartoons or movies were and still are your favorites?
  • What impact did Disney have on world cartoon and film culture?
  1. Theory: The Beginning of History and the Birth of Disney (3-5 minutes)
  2. Activity #2: Division into Breakout Rooms (5 minutes)
  • Each team should reflect on the topic a bit.: "Which of the classic Disney characters could be made a mascot besides Mickey Mouse?
  1. Theory: The Disney Era, successful projects (5-7 minutes)
  2. Activity #3 in Miro: Division into Breakout Rooms (3-5 minutes)
  • The board will feature iconic cartoons representing their Era.
  • It is necessary to combine a number of films with their own Era of "Disney".
  1.  Theory about the present and future legacy of "Disney": What is "Disney" now? (2-3 minutes)
  2. Activity #4: Quiz (5 minutes)
